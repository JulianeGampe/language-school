Our language levels

Language Levels
Our courses follow the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). You can find an overview on the Council of Europe Website.

We have broken down each level into two halves, each with two parts.
For example: Level B1 is set up with the following four courses: B1.1 Part 1, B1.1 Part 2, B1.2. Part 1, B1.2 Part 2.
If you are a complete beginner, please start with course A1.1 Part 1.
If you are unsure about your level, please feel free to contact us for more information.

Our course formats

Standard

Our standard courses take place once a week for 90 minutes over a period of 14 weeks. After that you will have acquired all necessary knowledge for the respective course level. This course format is for you if you prefer to complete a level over a longer period of time with more time for self-study.

Intensive

Our intensive courses take place one day a week over a period of 4 weeks. One course day consists of 7 lessons with 45 minutes each and usually has the following schedule, if not stated otherwise in the course description:
9:00-10:30
11:00-12:30
13:30-15:00
15:30-16:15
This course is for you if you want to make fast progress and if your are dedicated to learn the language in a short period of time.

Refresher

The refresher course is a one day course dedicated to refreshing the knowledge of a particular level. The course day has 5 lessons with 45 minutes each and usually has the following schedule, if not stated otherwise in the course description:
9:00-10:30
11:00-12:30
13:30-14:15
This course is for you if you have taken a longer break from learning German and you would like to fresh up your knowledge before starting with the next level.

Business German

Our business German courses are taught on Level B1 and Level B2 with a focus on business related topics and vocabulary. They take place once a week for 90 minutes over a period of 14 weeks. This course is for you if you are learning German especially for business purposes.

Holiday Course

Our holiday courses are taught on Level A1. They take place one day per week over a period of 4 weeks One course day consists of 5 lessons with 45 minutes each and usually has the following schedule, if not stated otherwise in the course description:
9:00-10:30
11:00-12:30
13:30-14:15
This course is for you if you do not know any German yet, but plan to go on a holiday. The course content is specifically focussed on vocabulary and conversations that you might come across on your next holiday.
